Overview

Our Basic 3D Modeling Workshop is designed to introduce beginners to the world of 3D design and digital fabrication. Over the course of this workshop, you'll learn the fundamental concepts and tools needed to create your own 3D models.

This hands-on workshop combines theory with practical exercises, giving you the chance to design your first 3D models and prepare them for printing. By the end of the workshop, you'll have created your own 3D model and will understand how to prepare it for production.

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand the basic principles of 3D modeling
  • Create and manipulate simple 3D shapes
  • Learn navigation and basic tools in popular 3D modeling software
  • Export files in the correct format for 3D printing
  • Understand the basics of 3D printing technology
  • Complete a small project from concept to 3D model

Workshop Structure

Part 1: Introduction to 3D Modeling

  • Introduction to 3D modeling concepts
  • Explain Software and setup
  • Interface overview and navigation
  • Creating basic shapes and transformations

Part 2: Practical Application

  • More advanced modeling techniques
  • Working with measurements and scale
  • Preparing models for 3D printing
  • Project work: designing your own object
  • Troubleshooting common issues

Projects

During this workshop, you'll complete the following projects:

Personal Nameplate

Create a custom nameplate or sign with engraving and cutting techniques.

Interlocking Design

Design and fabricate a 3D object using interlocking flat pieces.

Prerequisites

  • No prior experience with laser cutting required
  • Basic computer skills

Tools Used

  • CO2 laser cutter
  • Inkscape (free vector design software)
  • Various materials (wood, acrylic, cardboard)
  • Safety equipment
